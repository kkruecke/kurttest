How Civil War Draft worked in Indiana¶

The article Conscription and Draft in Indiana During the Civil War explains how the process of the Civil War draft, both the initial registration and the subsequent draft of some as new soldiers into the Army of the United States, was implemented at the county and township level in the state of Indiana.

Ship Passenger List Sites¶

Finding Passenger Lists & Immigration Records 1820-1940s. Arrivals at U.S. ports from Europe.

Web:

  1. Ancestry Search Page: New York, Passenger Lists, 1820-1957
  2. Castle Garden passenger list transcriptions.
  3. Steve Morse, which has powerful phoenic search.
  4. National Archives‘ German to America 1850-1897.
  5. immigrantships.net

Note

Bremen Departure lists from before 1920 no longer exist. They were destroyed in WWII.

Results of above searches on Familysearch using www.stevemorse.net

Emmanuel Lutheran Church Soest, 9909 Wayne Trace, Fort Wayne, IN¶

References below are from ALLEN COUNTY, INDIANA, CONGREGATIONAL RECORDS, HISTORIES, AND DIRECTORIES, IN THE GENEALOGY CENTER COLLECTION ALLEN COUNTY PUBLIC LIBRARY.

  1. Emmanuel Lutheran Church records, Marion Township, Allen County, Indiana, 4 vol. 1841-1973. 977.201 AL5em
  2. Minutes and family register, 1861-1925. 977.201 AL5eqa, oversized
    Contents: vol.1. Minutes, 1871-1925, vol.2. Constitution, minutes 1861-1873, family register.
  3. Histories. 977.201 AL5eq; 977.201 AL5elz
  4. Diary of F. W. Husmann. 977.201 AD1hu

Note

Husmann was the first ministry of the congregation.

Grace Evangelical Lutheran Church, 2940 S. Anthony Blvd., Fort Wayne, IN (ELCA)¶
