Heinrich “Henry” Behrman¶

Origin Discrepencies:¶

The Morrison Koenig Tree entry for Heinrich Friedrich August Beerman gives his place of origin as Ottenstein, Holzminden, Niedersachen, Germany whereas the early censuses give Brunswick or Braunschweig. Brunswick is the English equivalent of Braunschweig, a city in Niedersachsen located to the east of Hannover. Ottenstein is also in Niedersachsen, but it is not near Braunschweig. It is rather to the south of Hannover, near the south-eastern border of Niedersachsen.

Question: Was Brunswick/Braunschweig possibly larger in 1854?

Thoughts on Resolution of Arrival Discrepancy¶

The O Thijen/O Thyen Passenger List for New York arrival of Heinr Behrman is handwritten and not umabiguous. The arrival date of “June 23” could also be read “June 28”, and the year of 1854 could read as 1858. “Heinr” is also not real clear, either, though as good as any other guess.

We can take the entries for Fritz Behrman on this same O Thijen list and those of his family members and use his census date and those of his family members, as well as those of Heinrich and his family members and see if the calculated date of arrival from the censuses corresponds to 1854. Since we suspect that Fritz and Heinrich are related, we can see whether they both settled in Allen County or whether Fritz and his family attended Sankt Johannis Geheimnis or Soest Emmanual. We want a the census dates to correspond with the arrival date.

Frederich Behrman Issues¶

There is a Frederich Bearman, who I believe arrived on the same ship with Heinrich Behrman/Bearman, who lives in either Adams or Jefferson county, starting in at least , if I recall, 1880. He is still there, near Mary Bearman (Behrman) Gable and her husband Daniel Gable, as well as Louis (John L in census) Peppler.

In 1930 census, Mary K Bearman (or Behrman) Gable and Daniel Gable live next door to the son of Frederich Bearman. Louis C Bearman, and his wife Lena Bearman, their children and Frederich and Minnie Bearman: Mary K Bearman (Behrman) Gable and Daniel Gable live next door to the son of Frederich Bearman. Dolly Kikuchi’s Koch-Starke-Ahlschwede-et.al. tree’s entry for Heinrich Friedrich August Bearman cites Church Books of Liebfrauenkirche zu Ottenstein to document Heinrich Friedrich August Bearman’s birth on 17-Apr-1830, baptism on 25-Apri-1830, and marriage to Hanne Justine Louise Caroline Gieberl on 16-Mar-1854.
